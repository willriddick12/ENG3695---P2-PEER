USB (Universal Serial Bus) Drive Guide

The purpose for this guide is to inform individuals on how to operate a USB drive.

What is a USB?

A USB (Universal Serial Bus) drive is a device that is used for storing data. USB's includes an integrated interface along with flash memory. USB drives are built to be removable, rewritable, and durable. They tend to operate faster in large storage spaces and lack moving parts making them reliable. When plugged into a USB port, USB drives become accessible for storage. Other names for USB drive may also be flash drive or USB flash drive.

Main Components

  • Standard USB Plug - Connects the USB drive to a device.

  • USB Mass Storage Controller - Is a microcontroller for the USB that contains a small amount of RAM and ROM.

  • NAND Flash Memory Chip - A component where data is stored.

  • Crystal Oscillator - A component that controls the data output.

Steps for Use

1. Insert the flash drive into the USB port

First, locate the USB port on your device. USB ports are usually located on the back or side of your device, or on a desktop monitor. Now we can insert the drive. The connector must be lined up correctly with the computer's port. Look into the port and notice the bar (usually white/black/blue). Your USB will have a similar bar. Insert the drive so that these bars slide past one another. You will know if they collide if the USB does not slide fully in. Do not force this process as it may damage the drive.

2. Open the drive

Upon inserting the drive, you will likely be prompeted with a pop-up window indicating that the computer has found a new USB device. Click the pop-up to view the drive and the files it contains.

3. Locate your files

Locate the files you would like to transfer from your device to the drive. Select them and copy them with Control-C, and past them in your drive with Control-p. Additionally, you may remove or add files and folder to the USB drive if you would like to stay organize.

4. Close the files and eject the drive

Close files that are open on your computer. Right click on the USB drive and use the Eject command to disengage the USB from the computer. You may now safely remove the USB from your computer's USB port and store as you see fit

FAQs

  1. How much do USB flash drives cost?

    USB flash drives are priced based on their storage capacity. The price increases the more memory a flash drive has. For example, a standard 16 GB USB can cost about $6 while a 256 GB can cost about $35.

  2. What can be stored on a USB flash drive?

    Anything that you can store on your computer can be stored on your flash drive, provided it has enough storage capacity. Examples of things you can store on a flash drive:

    • Programs & Software
    • Pictures & Videos
    • Websites
    • Music
    • Work-related files

  3. Will my USB flash drive work with any computer?

    Yes, USB drives are compatible with any computer as long as they have USB ports.

  4. Where to purchase one?

    A good tip is to purchase one from a creditable manufacturer or online stores like:

    • Ebay
    • Amazon
    • Walmart
